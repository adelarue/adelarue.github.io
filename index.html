About me

I am a third-year PhD student at the MIT Operations Research Center, advised by Professor Dimitris Bertsimas. I am interested in new applications of mixed-integer optimization in transportation, machine learning, and public policy.

Over the last two years, I have worked on developing algorithms for routing school buses and optimizing school start times. With Dimitris Bertsimas and Sebastien Martin, we won first place in the Boston Public Schools Transportation Challenge in June 2017. You can read about the project in our paper, our SIAM News blog post or visualize BPS school buses in action here . Along with Boston Public Schools we were finalists for the 2019 Franz Edelman Award.

I am currently doing an internship in the Operations Research team at Google (Cambridge, MA).
